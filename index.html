Retail & CPG

    For a consumer products firm, trade promotion – that is, the collection of strategies and tactics used by a company to increase demand for products at the retail level – is the second biggest expense after cost of goods sold for a typical brand company, and ranges for most between 16 percent and 19 percent of annual revenue. Effective trade promotion management is a delicate balancing act that requires ongoing, real-time calculations of multiple variables.  If a detergent manufacturer offers a 10 cent rebate on every case of product a retailer buys, that retailer can be expected to buy more soap. And while the soap maker can forecast how much more the retailer will buy, the ability to quickly and accurately track and analyze how well the promotion is actually doing is imperative, and requires multiple systems to talk to each other.
